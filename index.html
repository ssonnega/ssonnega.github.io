How it works

Ski more. Spend less.

We've mapped every major US ski resort and ranked them by what matters most to budget-conscious skiers.

1

Browse the map

Color-coded markers show affordability at a glance. Green means budget-friendly, yellow is mid-range, red is premium.

2

Filter by what matters

Narrow by region, state, pass (Epic, Ikon, Indy & more), or set a max ticket price to find your perfect match.

3

Get full details — day ticket price, vertical drop, trail count, pass affiliation, and local tips for every mountain.

4

Plan your trip

Compare options side by side and find the best value within driving distance of where you are.

Price tiers

What does affordability mean?

We sort every resort into one of three tiers based on the walk-up day ticket price for the 2024–25 season.

💚

Budget

Under $100 / day

These resorts offer the best bang for your buck. Many are smaller, locally-owned mountains with fewer crowds and lower prices. Perfect for families and casual skiers.

🟡

Mid-Range

$100 – $160 / day

A solid balance of terrain, amenities, and price. Many of these resorts are on major ski passes, making them a great value if you buy a season pass in advance.

🔴

Expensive

Over $160 / day

Premium destination resorts with world-class terrain. Walk-up prices are steep, but Epic and Ikon pass holders can access many of these for a fraction of the cost.
